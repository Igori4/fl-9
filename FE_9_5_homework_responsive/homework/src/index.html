10 interesting facts about the Moon

The dark side of the Moon is myth.

  In reality both sides of the Moon see the same amount of sunlight however only one face of the Moon is ever seen from Earth.This is becouse the Moon rotates around on its own axis in exacly the same time it takes to orbit the Earth, meaning the same side is alweys facing the Earth. The side facing awey from Earth has only been seen by the human eye from spacecraft

The rise and fall of the tides on Earth is caused by the Moon.

  There are two buges in the Earth due to the gravitational pull that the Moon exerts; one on the side acing the Moon.and the other on the opposite side that faces away from the Moon. The bulges move around the oceans as the Earth rotates, causing high and low tides around the globe

The Moon is drifting away from the Earth.

  The Moon is moving approximately 3.8 cm away from our planet every year. It is estimated that it will countinue to do so for around 50 billion years. By the time that happens, the Moon will be taking around 47 days to orbit the Earth instead the current 27.3 days.

A person would weigh much less on the Moon.

  The Moon has much weaker gravity than Earth, due to its smaller mass, so tou would weigh about one sixth (16.5%) of your weight on Earth. This is why the lunar astronaits could leap and bound so high in the air

The Moon has only been walked on by 12 people; all American males.

  The first man to set foot on the Moon in 1969 was Neil Armstrong on the Apollo 11 mission, while the last man to walk on the Moon in 1972 was Gene Cernan on the Apollo 17 mission. Since then the Moon has only be visited by unmanned vehicles.

The Moon has no atmosphere.

  This means that the surface of the Moon is unprotected from cosmic rays, mereorites and solar winds, and has huge temperature variations. The lack of atmosphere means no sound can be heard on the Moon, and the sky always appears black.

The Moon has quakes.

  These are caused by the gravitational pull of the Earth. Lunar astronauts used seasmographs on their visits to the Moon, and found that small moonquakes occured several kilometres beneath the surface, causing ruptures and cracks. Scientists think the Moon has a molten core, ust like Earth.

The firs spacecraft to reach the Moon was Luna 1 in 1959.

  This was the Soviet craft, which was launched from the USSR. it passed within 5995 km of the surface of the Moon before going into orbit around the Sun.

The Moon will be visited by man in the near future.

  NASA plans to return astronauts to the moon to set up permanent space station. Mankind may once again walk on the moon in 2019. if all goes according to plan.
